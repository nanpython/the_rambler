Argument

Why do we take sides in an argument instead of exploring the topic deeply?

I have often found myself arguing, trying to prove that I am right. I try to convince the other person not only to see my point of view but to accept it as true. This attitude I have seen is behind every debate.

Debate is a duel, a clash of opinions, like the lancers of yore challenged to prove one’s superiority. A verbal duel is designed to accomplish the same thing: to prove that one’s point of view is superior to another’s, to show how clever one is.

When I have seen the true nature of debates between two persons or two groups, I have lost interest in debate per se. I may debate now and then out of habit, but I soon see the futility of it and drop it abruptly.

You cannot debate without taking sides. The clash of opinions generates the energy, not the passion to discover the truth of the matter.

Discussion is oriented towards discovery of facts of the matter. It is not hard to see this, when you are in a discussion group. Questions arise, there is a groping to find the answers, and you walk away with a twinkle in the eye. There is no winner or loser in a discussion.
