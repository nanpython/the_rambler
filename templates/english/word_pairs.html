Word Pairs

Accept | Except

Accept: hold as correct, receive or respond favorably
I accept defeat in the elections.
Except: excluding, apart from, omit
Everyone must go through military training, except the elderly and the physically disabled.

Anyway | Any way

Anyway: regardless, in any case, anyhow
I couldn't find the key to the lock; anyway, I am going to break it.
Any way: any manner, any method, any means
We can take any way to the park.

Compliment | Complement

Compliment: praise, admiration
She complimented him on his language skills.
Complement: something added to complete
The tie is a perfect complement to a suit.

Lose | Loose

Lose: misplace, fail to win, withdraw, suffer, shake off
She often loses her things because she does not remember where she left them.
Loose: not tight, sloppy, lax, informal
I prefer loose clothing.

Who | Whom

Who: Subject in a sentence, used as a pronoun
Who is going to get the children to the party?
Whom: Object in a sentence, used as a pronoun
You assigned this project to whom?
